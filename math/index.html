Definitions=

  1. Parallel Lines: Lines with the same slope on the same plane that never intersect
  2. Perpendicular Lines: Lines with opposite reciprocal slopes on the same plane that intersect to form 90° angles
  3. Line Segments: Lines bounded by two endpoints that contain every point on the line between its endpoints
  4. Theorem: A statement that can be proved
  5. Circle: An arc where all points on the arc are equidistant from a center point
  6. Angle: Two lines or rays diverging to form a common point
  7. Centroid: The point where all 3 medians intersect and is often described as the triangle's center of gravity
  8. Circumcenter: The point where the perpendicular bisectors of the sides intersect
  9. Incenter: The point where the three angle bisectors are always concurrent and always meet in the triangle's interior
  10. Orthocenter: The point where all three altitudes of the triangle intersect
  11. Bisector: A line that cuts another line into two equal parts
  12. Midsegment: The line segment in a triangle that splits the two sides into two equal parts and has the same slope as the base
  13. Transversal: A line that cuts through parallel lines
Transformations=

The equation for a transformation is y = f(x - h)n + k, where f represents the growth factor, h represents a shift on the x-axis, n represents the rate of change [not part of a transformation, only used for polynomial functions (quadratic, cubic, etc)], and k represents a shift on the y-axis.

Translations=
A translation is the shifting of a figure on a plane.
To shift the figure, the equation is T(x) = (x - h) + k, where h is the shift on the x-axis and k is the shift on the y-axis.
The expression for a translation is T(x + h, y + k), where h is the shift on the x-axis and k is the shift on the y-axis.

Rotations=
If center of rotation is the origin; cRot(0, 0):
90° CCW or 270° CW: P(x, y) → Rot(-y, x)
180° CCW or 180° CW: P(x, y) → Rot(-x, -y)
270° CCW or 90° CW: P(x, y) → Rot(y, -x)

If the center of rotation is not the origin; cRot(a, b):
Change P(x, y) to P(m, n), where m represents the x-point - a, and n represents the y-point - b
90° CCW or 270° CW: P(m, n) → Rot(-n+b, m+a)
180° CCW or 180° CW: P(m, n) → Rot(-m+a, -n+b)
270° CCW or 90° CW: P(x, y) → Rot(n+b, -m+a)

Rotational Symmetry=
  • The rotational symmetry of a regular polygon is found by dividing 360/n
  • A regular polygon is a polygon in which all of the sides and angles are congruent
  • Therefore, the rotational symmetry of an equilateral triangle is 120° because 360/3 is 120
  • As well, the rotational symmetry for a square is 90° because 360/4 is 90
  • Also, the rotational symmetry for a pentagon is 72° because 360/5 is 72
  • Thus, the rotational symmetry for a hexagon is 60° because 360/6 is 60
  • You can rotate a regular polygon whatever number is multiple of its rotational symmetry
Line Symmetry=
  • Line symmetry is when you can cut a shape in half and both sides are exact reflections of each other

Reflections=
A reflection is the creation of a mirror figure over a line.
The formula for a reflection over the x-axis is P(x, y) → Ref(x, -y).
The formula for a reflection over the y-axis is P(x, y) → Ref(-x, y).
A reflection over the line y = x is same as a 180° rotation.

Dilation=
A dilation is the change in a figure's size. This type of transformation creates similar figures, while the others create congruent figures.
The formula for a dilation is D(x, y) = (f*x, f*y), where f is the dilation factor.

The center of dilation is a fixed point on a plane that corresponds to the pre-image; if cDil(0, 0) and (0, 0) is the same as point A, then point A' on the after-image will be at (0, 0).
The distance between the center of dilation and any point on the after-image is the original distance between the center and the point on the pre-image multiplied by the scale factor.

Pythagorean Theorem=
The Pythagorean Theorem is a theorem that states that a right triangle's side lengths MUST meet this setup: a2 + b2 = c2
The formula for the Pythagorean Theorem is a2 + b2 = c2, where a is the short leg, b is the long leg, and c is the hypotenuse.

Here are the steps for using the Pythagorean Theorem:
Step 1: Find the measure of the two legs and the hypotenuse
Step 2: Insert the measures of the sides into the formula (if the two legs are the same length, just use 2*a2 = c2)
Step 3: Simplify to find c

Example:
Step 1: a = 3, b = 4, c = ?
Step 2: (3)2 + (4)2 = c2
Step 3: 9 + 16 = c2 → 25 = c2 → 5 = c

Congruent v. Similar Figures=

Congruent figures have the same size, shape, and angle measures.
Similar figures have corresponding sides that are proportional and the same shape and angle measures.
