Jenga Review

Questions

  1. Where did final authority in all matters belong under the Articles of Confederation?
    1. The President
    2. Congress
    3. The Supreme Court
    4. The States
    5. None of the Above
    Answer: The States (D)

  2. Shay's Rebellion demonstrated that:
    1. Local militias could put down rebellions
    2. The new national government might interfere with the rights of citizens
    3. The Articles of Confederation were not working
    4. The army was too weak to fight
    5. All of the Above
    Answer: The Articles of Confederation were not working (C)

  3. All of the following were weaknesses under the Articles of Confederation EXCEPT:
    1. There was no way to settle disputes between states
    2. The national government could not enforce treaties with other countries
    3. The Congress could not tax to raise its own money
    4. The national government was infringing on the rights of the individual states
    5. A huge national debt and cash shortage had piled up during the war
    Answer: The national government was infringing on the rights of the individual states (B)

  4. Which of the following ideas is NOT associated with John Locke?
    1. All people have a natural right to life, liberty, and property
    2. Legislative, executive, and judicial powers should be divided among three branches of government
    3. The government has the duty to protect the natural rights of citizens
    4. Government is based on an agreement between the ruler and the ruled
    Answer: Legislative, executive, and judicial powers should be divided among three branches of government (B)

  5. Some people opposed the Constitution because they believed that it:
    1. Gave the President too much power
    2. Gave the states too much power
    3. Made the people pay taxes that were too high
    4. Did not provide adequate representation for the people
    5. All of the Above
    Answer: Did not provide adequate representation for the people (D)

  6. The most serious criticism that the Anti-federalists directed against the Constitution was that it:
    1. Could not be amended
    2. Failed to give enough power to the president
    3. Gave too much power to the judicial branch
    4. Lacked a bill of rights
    5. Did away with the Articles of Confederation
    Answer: Lacked a bill of rights (D)

  7. Which of the following reasons was NOT a criticism that the Anti-federalists made of the Constitution?
    1. There was no bill of rights
    2. The Constitutional Convention had exceeded its authority by writing a totally new constitution instead of simply amending the Articles of Confederation
    3. The central government would be too strong at the expense of the states
    4. A small group could become too powerful and be worse than a monarch
    5. There wouldn't be a standing army
    Answer: There wouldn't be a standing army (E)

  8. This group felt that a Bill of Rights was unnecessary because the national government was limited to the powers granted to it in the Constitution.
    1. Federalists
    2. Anti-federalists
    3. Both Federalists and Anti-federalists
    Answer: Federalists (A)

  9. Which of the following is the only power that the Constitution does NOT give to Congress?
    1. Borrow money
    2. Provide for the common defense
    3. Power to create a Social Security system
    4. Power to establish post offices
    Answer: Power to create a Social Security system (C)

  10. The amendment process is carried out by which of the following?
    1. Direct vote of the people
    2. Congress and the state legislatures
    3. Congress and the President
    4. The electoral college
    5. The Supreme Court
    Answer: Congress and the President (C)
