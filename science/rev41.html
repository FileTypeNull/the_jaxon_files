Questions

1. How are ATP and ADP related?
Answer: They are both energy sources.

2. What types of molecules are broken down to make ATP? Which are most often broken down to make ATP?
Answer: Food molecules, but specifically glucose.

3. How are some organisms able to survive without sunlight and photosynthesis?
Answer: Some use chemical energy.

4. Describe how you do not get energy directly from the food that you eat?
Answer: Food needs to be digested and broken down into smaller parts. These smaller parts can be eventually broken down into ATP

5. How are the energy needs of plant cells similar to those of animal cells? How are they different?
Answer: Plants also need ATP, but they do not ingest or digest food.

6. A water molecule is added to an ATP molecule to break down ATP into ADP and a phosphate group. Write the chemical equation that represents this reaction.
Answer: H2O + C10H16N5O13P3 → C10H18N5O14P3
