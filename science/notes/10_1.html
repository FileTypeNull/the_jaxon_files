Early Ideas About Evolution

Notes

Early Scientists Proposed Ideas about Evolution=
  • Evolution is the process of biological change by which descendants come to differ from their ancestors
  • Carolus Linnaeus=
    1. Linnaeus was a botanist in the 1700s who developed a classification system for all types of organisms known at the time
    2. Although he grouped organisms by their similarities, the system also reflects evolutionary relationships
    3. He proposed that, instead of being fixed and unchanging, some organisms might have been created through the crossing of species
    4. A species is a group of organisms so similar to one another that they can reproduce and have fertile offspring
  • Georges Louis Leclerc de Buffon=
    1. Buffon was a French naturalist of the 1700s and claimed that all species came from a single, common ancestor
    2. He also argued that Earth was far older than 6,000 years old
  • Erasmus Darwin=
    1. Erasmus Darwin is the grandfather of Charles Darwin, who is considered the father of evolution
    2. He proposed that the all living things were descended from a common ancestor that more-complex forms of life arose from less-complex forms
  • Jean-Baptiste Lamarck=
    1. Lamarck proposed that all organisms evolved toward perfection and complexity
    2. Lamarck believed in the idea of inheritance of acquired characteristics

Theories of Geologic Change Set the Stage for Darwin's Theory=
  • French zoologist Georges Cuvier discovered fossils, or traces of organisms that existed in the past, in each layer of rock
  • He found that the fossils in the deepest layers were quite different from those in the upper layers, which were formed by more recent layers of sediment
  • The theory of catastrophism states that natural disasters such as floods and volcanic eruptions have been happened often during Earth's long history
  • Cuvier argued that the appearance of new species in each rock layer resulted from other species' moving into the area from elsewhere after each catastrophic event
  • James Hutton proposed that the changes he observed in landforms resulted from slow changes over a long period of time, a principle that became known as gradualism
  • Lyell expanded Hutton's theory of gradualism into the theory of uniformitarianism, which states that the geologic processes that shape Earth are uniform through time

Questions

  1. Briefly describe two ideas about evolution that were proposed by scientists in the 18th century.
    Answer: Buffon believed that different species had come from a common ancestor and Darwin's grandfather proposed that more-complex forms of life arose from less-complex forms of life.
  2. What ideas about Lyell's theory of uniformitarianism were important for evolutionary theory?
    Answer: The aspect of Lyell's theory that is most important is that evolution occurs gradually throughout time at a constant rate.
