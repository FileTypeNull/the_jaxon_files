DNA Fingerprinting

Notes

A DNA Fingerprint is a Type of Restriction Map=
  • A DNA fingerprint is a representation of parts of an individual's DNA that can be used to identify a person at the molecular level
  • A DNA fingerprint is a specific type of restriction map
  • The greatest differences in DNA among people are those found in regions of the genome that are not parts of genes
  • DNA fingerprinting focuses on the noncoding regions of DNA, or DNA sequences outside of the genes
DNA Fingerprinting is Used for Identification=
  • The specific nucleotide sequences that are repeated can be found in everyone
  • From one person to another, the number of repeat sequences can differ greatly, even among brothers and sisters
  • DNA Fingerprints and Probability=
    1. Identification with DNA fingerprinting depends on probability
    2. Individual probabilities are multiplied by each other to find the total probability
    3. DNA fingerprinting compares at least five regions of the genome which makes patterns unique
    4. The more DNA that is studied, the less likely it becomes that two people share the same DNA fingerprint
  • Uses of DNA Fingerprinting=
    1. DNA fingerprints are often used in legal cases
      • Evidence can come from even the smallest of samples due to PCR
      • Sometimes DNA fingerprints are used against a suspect, but other times they are used to prove someone's innocence
    2. DNA fingerprints can prove family relationships and are used to study biodiversity

Questions

  1. On what, in a person's DNA, is a DNA fingerprint based?
    Answer: A person's DNA fingerprint is based on the part of the genome that does not contain genes.

  2. Describe two ways in which DNA fingerprinting is used.
    Answer: DNA fingerprinting can be used to prove family relationships and prove someone's innocence.
