Evidence of Evolution

Notes

Evidence for Evolution in Darwin's Time Came from Several Sources=
  • The most important and convincing support for evolution came from fossils, geography, embryology, and anatomy:
  • Fossils=
    1. Even before Darwin, scholars studying fossils knew that organisms changed over time
    2. Scientists who study fossils also think about its age, its location, and what the environment was like when the organism was alive
    3. In the late 1700s, geologists wondered why certain types of fossils were found in some layers of rock and not others
    4. Later studies suggest that the fossil organisms at the bottom, or older, layers were more primitive than those in the upper, or newer, layers
  • Geography=
    1. Biogeography is the study of the distribution of organisms around the world
  • Embryology=
    1. The embryos of vertebrates can be hard to tell apart
    2. The similar features of embryos in very different organisms suggest evolution from a distant common ancestor
  • Anatomy=
    1. Homologous structures are features that are similar in structure but appear in different organisms and have different functions
    2. The most common examples of homologous structures are the forelimbs of tetrapod vertebrates
    3. Analogous structures are structures that perform a similar function but are not similar in origin

Questions

  1. Name the four sources of evidence for evolution upon which Darwin based his ideas on common descent
    Answer: Fossils, Geography, Embryology, Anatomy

  2. Why are vestigial structures considered critical evidence for evolution?
    Answer: It allows us to see that some things that organisms have now that are useless originally had a function, and we can see how and why evolution shaped that organism in that manner.
