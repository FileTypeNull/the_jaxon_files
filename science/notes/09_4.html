Genetic Engineering

Notes

Entire Organisms can be Cloned=
  • A clone is a genetically identical copy of a gene or of an organism
  • Clones can occur naturally
  • Mammals are cloned through a technique called nuclear transfer
    1. First, an unfertilized egg is taken from an animal and is enucleated
    2. Then, the nucleus of the cell from the animal to be cloned is implanted into the egg
    3. The egg is stimulated and, if the procedure is successful, the egg will begin dividing
    4. After the embryo grows for a few days, it is transplanted into a female
  • Cloning is also controversial because the success rate for cloning mammals is very low and the successful subjects are occasionally more susceptible to disease than the original
New Genes can be Added to an Organism's DNA=
  • The changing of an organism's DNA to give the organism new traits is called genetic engineering
  • Genetic engineering is based on the use of recombinant DNA technologies
  • Recombinant DNA is DNA that contains genes from more than one organism
  • Recombinant DNA could be used to produce crop plants that make medicines and vitamins
  • Plasmids are closed loops of DNA that are separate from the bacterial chromosome and that replicate on their own within the cell

Genetic Engineering Produces Organisms with New Traits=
  • A transgenic organism has one or more genes from another organism inserted into its genome
  • Genetic Engineering in Plants=
    1. The genetic engineering of plants is directly related to the genetic engineering of bacteria
    2. After the bacteria infect the plant, the new gene becomes a part of the plant's DNA and is expressed like any other gene
    3. This technique allowed scientists to give plants new traits, such as resistance to frost, diseases, and insects
  • Genetic Engineering in Animals=
    1. Transgenic animals are much harder to produce than plants
    2. Mice are often used as models for humans
    3. Some mice, known as gene knockout mice, are made by disrupting the function of genes
