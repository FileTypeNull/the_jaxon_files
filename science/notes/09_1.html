Manipulating DNA

Notes

Scientists Use Several Techniques to Manipulate DNA=
  • By the 1950s, scientists had concluded that DNA was the genetic material
  • Watson and Crick had determined the structure of DNA
Restriction Enzymes Cut DNA=
  • A whole chromosome is too large for scientists to study a particular gene, so they had to find a way to get smaller pieces of DNA
  • Restriction enzymes are enzymes that cut DNA molecules at specific nucleotide sequences
  • The sequence of nucleotides that is identified and cut by a restriction enzyme is called a restriction site
  • They are called 'restriction' enzymes because they 'restrict', or decrease, the risk of a virus on a bacterial cell
  • Every restriction enzyme has a different restriction site and will cut the same DNA molecule in different ways
  • Blunt ends are formed when restriction enzymes cut DNA straight across
  • Sticky ends are formed from cuts that leave tails of free DNA bases on each side of the cut
Restriction Maps Show the Lengths of DNA Fragments=
  • In gel electrophoresis, and electrical current is used to separate a mixture of DNA fragments from each other
  • The length of a DNA fragment can be determined using gel electrophoresis
  • Restriction maps show the lengths of DNA fragments between restriction sites in a strand of DNA
  • Restriction maps can also be used to study gene mutations

Questions

  1. List four different ways in which scientists can manipulate DNA.
    Answer: PCR, gel electrophoresis, restriction enzymes, and restriction maps are all ways that scientists can manipulate DNA.

  2. What determines how DNA will be cut by a restriction enzymes?
    Answer: DNA will be cut based on what restriction enzymes cut it and how many restriction sites it has.

  3. How does gel electrophoresis separate DNA fragments from each other?
    Answer: Gel electrophoresis separates DNA fragments from each other based on the lengths between restriction sites.
