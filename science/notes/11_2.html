Natural Selection in Populations

Notes

Natural Selection Acts on Distributions of Traits=
  • Normal distribution is a type of distribution in which the frequency is highest near the mean value and decreases toward each extreme end of the range
  • When these frequency values are graphed, the result is a bell-shaped curve
Natural Selection Can Change the Distribution of a Trait in One of Three Ways=
  • Microevolution is the observable change in the allele frequencies of a population over time
  • Microevolution occurs on a small scale within a single population
  • Natural selection leads to microevolution
  • Directional Selection=
    1. A type of selection that favors phenotypes at one extreme of a trait's range is called directional selection
    2. Directional selection causes a shift in a population's phenotypic distribution—an extreme phenotype that was once rare in a population becomes more common
  • Stabilizing Selection=
    1. During stabilizing selection, the intermediate phenotype is favored and becomes more common in the population; it becomes stable at the intermediate phenotype rather than shifting toward one of the extremes
    2. Stabilizing selection increases the number of individuals with intermediate phenotypes
    3. In some populations, the extreme phenotypes may be lost altogether
  • Disruptive Selection=
    1. Disruptive selection occurs when both extreme phenotypes are favored, while individuals with intermediate phenotypes are selected against by something in nature

Questions

  1. In terms of phenotypes, describe what is meant by the phrase "distribution of traits."
    Answer: The distribution of traits refers to the chance for a specific allele to be distributed throughout a population.

  2. What are three ways in which natural selection can change a distribution of traits?
    Answer: Stabilizing Selection, Disruptive Selection, Directional Selection
