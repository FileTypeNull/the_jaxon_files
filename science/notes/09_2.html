Copying DNA

Notes

PCR Uses Polymerases to Copy DNA=
  • Scientists need to get a large enough sample of DNA to study and manipulate it
  • Polymerase chain reaction (PCR) is a technique that produces millions—or even billions—of copies of a specific DNA sequence in just a few hours
    1. Kary Mullis invented PCR, and he decided that heat should be used to separate the DNA strands instead of enzymes
    2. PCR uses polymerases that come from bacteria which live in temperatures of up to 80°C to copy the DNA strands
PCR is a Three-Step Process=
  • PCR uses four materials: a DNA template, DNA polymerases, artificial nucleotides, and two primers
  • A primer is a short segment of DNA that acts as the starting point for a new strand
  • Primers are needed in PCR because DNA polymerases can add nucleotides to strands that have already been started, but cannot start the strand
  • PCR has three main steps:
    1. Separating: The container with all of the reactants is heated to separate the double-stranded DNA into single strands
    2. Binding: The container is cooled and the primers bind to their complementary DNA sequences
    3. Copying: The container is heated again and the polymerases keep adding nucleotides until the sequence is complete
    This sequence is repeated multiple times until there is enough DNA for the scientists to observe (doubles every cycle)
