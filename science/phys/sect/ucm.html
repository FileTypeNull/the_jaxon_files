Universal Circular Motion

Centripetal Acceleration

Acceleration can occur in two situations: there is either a change in speed or a change in direction. When there is a change in direction yet a constant speed that is tangential to the net force of the object, the object is experiencing centripetal acceleration, or ac.
Know that: ac = v²⁄r
Imagine that a point is travelling along a circle at a constant rate, but is still accelerating due to centripetal acceleration around some point:

This is similar to the motion of a planet around a circular orbit. This is because the only force affecting this object, assuming no friction, is the force that points to the center of orbit. In a real-life application, imagine tying a ball to a rope and swinging the rope around your head. The ball travels in a circular motion around the center: your head due to the tension force applied by the rope on the ball.
