Term (p.o.s.) Definition Synonyms/Antonyms Sentences
Admonish (v.) to warn or firmly reprimand Syn: rebuke, chastise, urge against

Ant: endorse, praise 		Aykesano admonished Tetsu's attempt to defuse the bomb as he had no understanding of basic circuitry.
Articulate (v.) to express (an idea or feeling) fluently and coherently Syn: vocalize, enunciate, state

Ant: stifle, misrepresent, disconnect 		In order to effectively get his/her message across, a person giving a speech must articulate the words so that the audience can understand and piece the information together clearly.
Assert (v.) to declare or strongly express (an idea or feeling) Syn: contend, argue, proclaim

Ant: question, opinionate, deny 		Akuryō asserted his belief that it is in fact possible for pigs to fly if they are given mechanical enhancements.
Belittle (v.) to make (someone/something) out as unimportant Syn: denigrate, degrade, trivialize

Ant: praise, honor, admire 		The girls belittled him because he was interested in academics and maintained a low profile in social situations, which was in direct opposition to their lifestyles.
Concede (v.) to admit that something is true and valid (after first denying its legitimacy) Syn: acknowledge, confess, surrender

Ant: deny, argue 		Hayai conceded to Kiyo in their argument regarding the higher quality clothing line, which made Kiyo the victor.
Differentiate (v.) to ascertain what makes (someone/something) different Syn: distinguish, separate

Ant: compare, familiarize 		It's easy to differentiate between the issues of slavery and racism.
Dismiss (v.) deliberately cease to think about Syn: banish, disregard, shrug off

Ant: entertain, consider 		Dan dismissed his thoughts of self-depreciation and decided to live his life to the fullest, starting tomorrow.
Evoke (v.) to bring or recall Syn: elicit, induce, stimulate

Ant: ignore, repress, stifle 		I did not mean to evoke any feelings of depression from your past.
Exaggerate (v.) to represent something as more profound than in reality Syn: emphasize, amplify, overestimate

Ant: understate, minimize 		Dude, stop exaggerat(e)ing; you really don't have that much homework.
Expose (v.) to reveal the true and typically objectionable nature of (someone or something) Syn: reveal, uncover, make known

Juxtapose (v.) to place adjacently for contrasting effect Syn: collocate, compare/contrast

Reinforce (v.) to strengthen (an existing idea, belief, or feeling) Syn: fortify, support

Ant: weaken, reduce, diminish 		The construction workers reinforced the building's structural integrity with steel bars.
Ridicule (v.) to mock with dismissive language or behavior Syn: mock, deride, scorn

Ant: praise, popularize 		She ridiculed Charles because of his lackadaisical demeanor and ecstatic personality.
Suggest (v.) to put forward for consideration Syn: recommend, propose, advocate

Ant: reject, oppose 		I suggest that you be quiet before you say something you might regret.

Ant: commend, promote, support 		Donald Trump protected Kavanaugh from the abusive women who attempted to vilify his character.
