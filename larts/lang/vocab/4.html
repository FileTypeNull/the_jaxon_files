amicable (adj.) characterized by goodwill Syn: friendly, peacable, cooperative

Ant: hostile, antagonistic, unfriendly 		Jim and Jill decided to keep their divorce amicable to avoid fighting would negatively affect their children.

Ant: peace, organization, serenity 		The anarchy that ensued after the death of Abraham Lincoln would not be calmed until Andrew Johnson became president.

Ant: commonality 		Since water's solid state, ice, is less dense than its liquid form, water is a chemical anomaly as this characteristic is due to rare hydrogen bonding.

Ant: modern, common 		The tomb was covered in archaic symbols that the archaeologists could not decipher since it had died out thousands of years ago.

Ant: verbosity, length 		The brevity of the Gettysburg Address allowed Lincoln to change the course of the war in all but a few words.

Ant: slowing agent, effect 		In chemistry, a catalyst speeds up the rate of the reaction and is not consumed in the overall reaction.
