Term (p.o.s.) Definition Synonyms/Antonyms Sentences
impotent (adj.) lacking in power or strength Syn: weak, feeble, fragile

Ant: strong, stable, potent 		The crowd knew that the impotent boxer would not be able to beat the returning champion, Rocky Balboa.
antithesis (n.) the complete opposite Syn: reverse, flip side

Ant: copy 		Chrissie is the antithesis of Ben because she is friendly to strangers while he spits in their face and curses at them for invading his personal space.
maelstrom (n.) a state or situation of extreme or violent turmoil Syn: chaos, disarray, confusion

Ant: serenity, peace 		Vicky caused a maelstrom of panic when she told everyone that there had been a mass shooting across from the school.
emendation (n.) the process of making corrections to a text Syn: revision, editing

Ant: draft, keep the same 		When Dave submitted a term paper that required emendations, the teacher asked him to take another week to revise any errors in his submission.
chagrin (n.) distress or embarrassment Syn: vexation, exasperation, humiliation

Ant: pride, delight 		Todd's parents did not hide their chagrin after their son had a hissy fit in the local Walmart, screaming and shouting for everyone to hear.
bauble (n.) a small trinket or decoration Syn: toy, ornament, knickknack

Ant: tool 		Mary scolded Bert for wasting money on useless baubles such as a small magnet and marbles.
diaphanous (adj.) light or translucent Syn: delicate, flimsy

Ant: opaque, thick 		Ashley was suspended for wearing a diaphanous shirt to class when it slightly revealed her undergarments.
labyrinth (n.) a maze or problem Syn: puzzle, web, complication

Ant: order, simplicity, organization 		The mall is a labyrinth; check the map or you will definitely get lost.
gloat (v.) to brag Syn: crow, rejoice, rub it in

Ant: commiserate, sympathize, chagrin 		Heidi's friends did not want to hear her gloat about her 100 on the last exam while they only scored in the low 70s.
impediment (n.) a hindrance or obstruction Syn: obstacle, barrier, restriction

Ant: advantage, blessing, facilitation 		The largest impediment to crossing a river is the current, since it could push you downstream and away from your destination.
bestial (adj.) savagely cruel; of or like an animal Syn: savage, brutish, barbarous

Ant: humane, civilized 		The soldier endured bestial treatment after he was captured by the rebels and interrogated for information.
effete (adj.) having lost character, vitality, or strength Syn: weakened, enervated, feeble

Ant: powerful, strong 		The team must change its effete strategy if they want to win the game.
shard (n.) a broken piece, normally with pointed or jagged edges Syn: fragment, splinter, chip

Ant: whole, base, core 		Shards of broken taillights littered the highway after the major car collision that crumpled nearly a dozen cars.
bland (adj.) lacking strong flavor Syn: insipid, tasteless, plain

Ant: flavorful, tasty, savory 		Your dinner will taste bland if you do not season the food with spices and other ingredients.
nihilism (n.) the belief that all is null Syn: cynicism, pessimism, skepticism

Ant: optimism, religion 		People knew that Jimmy practicied nihilism when he argued that religion was pointless and life was meaningless.
