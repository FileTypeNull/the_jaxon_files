Term (p.o.s.) Definition Synonyms/Antonyms Sentences
tone (n.) the expression of the rhetor's attitude through the text Syn: expression, feelings

Ant: facts 		The tone of In Cold Blood is one of dark, enchanting mystery regarding the series of events that led to the killing of the Clutter family.
apprehensive (adj.) anxious that something bad will occur Syn: worried, nervous

Ant: excited 		Rachel was apprehensive to breathe near the red sweater as its foul smell was too putrid.
arduous (adj.) requiring effort Syn: tiring, strenuous

Ant: easy, relaxing 		Malcolm Gladwell declares, in his book Outliers, that success will come first from heritage and then arduous merit.
brash (adj.) self-assertive in an overbearing way Syn: self-confident, arrogant

Ant: self-hating 		Tom Buchanan was brash, violent, and bold; yet, he was also smart and cautious.
colloquial (adj.) used in common conversation Syn: informal

Ant: formal 		"Hills Like White Elephants" was a very colloquial text, as it was primarily informal dialogue between the two characters.
contemplative (adj.) expressing or involving complex thought Syn: thinking, cautious

Ant: easygoing 		Gatsby, for the past five years, has been very contemplative about his plan to win over Daisy.
facetious (adj.) treating serious issues with inappropriate humor Syn: sarcastic, flippant

Ant: serious 		Michio Kaku was often very facetious throughout his novel Physics of the Future, including bits of physics jokes that were sarcastic in nature.
flippant (adj.) not showing a serious or respectful attitude Syn: facetious, satirical

Ant: serious, respectful 		Athough Perry was quite solemn about the situation, Dick treated the murders with a flippant character.
listless (adj.) lacking energy or enthusiasm Syn: lethargic, unethusiastic

Ant: excited, ecstatic 		Lee Sedol, having lost his matches against AlphaGo, came back listless, uninterested, and depressed.
malicious (adj.) characteristically bad in nature Syn: evil, toxic

Ant: good 		Dick Hickcock was a man with a malicious temperment and scornful demeanor.
morose (adj.) sullen and ill-tempered Syn: glum, gloomy

Ant: happy, joyous 		Fan Hui became quite morose after his defeat to AlphaGo; however, he eventually realized that this was a unique event and that he was happy to have contributed to such a revolutionary concept.
optimistic (adj.) hopeful for the future Syn: cheerful, confident

Ant: pessimistic 		Gatsby was optimistic that his plan to win over Daisy and steal her from Tom would work out perfectly.
restrained (adj.) held back Syn: restricted

Ant: free, loose 		Myrtle felt restrained by her husband George Wilson, and sought a more carefree life with Tom.
satirical (adj.) sarcastic Syn: mocking, cynical

Ant: caring, serious 		None of the books we have read this year have been satirical.
solemn (adj.) charcacterized by deep sincerity Syn: somber, serious

Ant: satirical, flippant 		The solemn tone of In Cold Blood makes the story much more impactful in a literary sense.
